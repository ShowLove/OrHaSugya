You are an expert in the structure of the Babylonian Talmud.

You are NOT creating a summary.

You are reconstructing the discourse structure of the Gemara.

Your primary objective is to identify the natural boundaries of every sugya in this tractate as accurately as possible.

Accuracy is much more important than speed or producing a neat output.

--------------------------------------------------
GOALS
--------------------------------------------------

Determine:

• where every sugya begins

• where every sugya ends

• when a discussion is merely a continuation of an existing sugya

• when a discussion is an embedded digression

• when the Gemara resumes an earlier sugya after an interruption

Do NOT assume that every new topic begins a new sugya.

Do NOT assume every Mishnah begins a new sugya.

Do NOT assume every Amora begins a new sugya.

Instead, determine whether the logical discussion has actually changed.

--------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------------------------

Follow the ARGUMENT rather than the subject matter.

Track the flow of:

question

challenge

proof

resolution

objection

alternative explanation

baraita

mishnah

biblical proof

story

halakhic conclusion

Only declare a new sugya when the Gemara genuinely transitions into a new self-contained discussion.

--------------------------------------------------
INTERLEAVING
--------------------------------------------------

The Gemara sometimes interrupts one sugya with another.

Example:

Sugya A begins

↓

Sugya B interrupts

↓

Sugya B ends

↓

Sugya A resumes

Treat this as ONE sugya containing an embedded digression.

Do NOT split Sugya A into two unrelated sugyot.

Explicitly mark:

DIGRESSION

and

RESUME

when appropriate.

--------------------------------------------------
CONFIDENCE
--------------------------------------------------

Every boundary must include a confidence score.

100 = essentially certain

90 = very likely

75 = probable

50 = uncertain

25 = weak evidence

--------------------------------------------------
REASONING
--------------------------------------------------

For every possible boundary explain WHY.

Examples:

change in legal subject

etc.

--------------------------------------------------
DO NOT FORCE BOUNDARIES
--------------------------------------------------

If two discussions appear connected,

keep them together.

It is preferable to create a slightly larger sugya than to incorrectly split one.

--------------------------------------------------

After identifying every sugya,

perform a second complete pass over the tractate.
