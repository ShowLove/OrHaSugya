# Sugya Argument Analysis Prompt v3

You are an expert in the structure of the Babylonian Talmud.

You are given THREE files.

FILE 1

berakhot_sugya_analysis_pass1.json

This file contains the proposed sugya boundaries.

FILE 2

berakhot_bundle.jsonl

This file contains the original text of the tractate.

FILE 3

berakhot_authority_analysis_all_sugyot.json

This file contains the authority structure of each sugya in terms of sources cited and how each source functions inside the argument.

Your purpose is NOT to redefine the sugyot.

Instead, analyze every sugya independently according to the supplied sugya boundaries.

The purpose of this analysis is to reconstruct the complete ARGUMENT STRUCTURE of every sugya.

====================================================

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE

====================================================

For every sugya:

• identify every distinct argument

• identify every side of every argument

• identify every participant

• identify every supporting source

• determine how each source supports each argument

• identify objections

• identify resolutions

• identify unresolved disputes

• identify practical conclusions

• identify narrative lessons when the sugya is primarily aggadic

• provide bounded details that explain the concrete content of the issue, question, positions, reasoning, underlying principles, and conclusions

The final output must be ONE valid JSON object.

====================================================

This makes the JSON consistent and easier to analyze later.

====================================================

IMPORTANT DETAILS REQUIREMENT

====================================================

Every argument must include bounded explanatory details.

These details are meant to answer:

“What exactly does this mean in context?”

For example, do not merely say:

"Rabban Gamliel’s practical ruling."

Instead explain:

"Rabban Gamliel rules that even though the Rabbis say evening Shema may be recited only until midnight, one who failed to recite it before midnight may still recite it until dawn."

Details should be specific enough to understand the argument without rereading the full sugya.

But details must remain compact.

====================================================

DETAIL QUALITY RULES

====================================================

Bad detail:

"Rabban Gamliel gives a practical ruling."

Good detail:

"Rabban Gamliel tells his sons that they may still recite evening Shema before dawn, because the underlying time extends until dawn even though the Rabbis set midnight as a safeguard."

Bad detail:

"The Gemara asks about the source."

Good detail:

"The Gemara asks why the Mishnah begins with evening Shema instead of morning Shema, since the Torah phrase 'when you rise' might imply beginning with morning."

Bad detail:

"One side says yes."

Good detail:

"This side holds that the Mishnah follows the order of creation, where evening precedes morning."

Bad status.details:

"Multiple Valid Opinions."

Good status.details:

"The sugya preserves three positions: Rabbi Eliezer limits the time to the first watch, the Rabbis set midnight as a safeguard, and Rabban Gamliel permits recitation until dawn."

Bad accepted_position.details:

"Rabban Gamliel is accepted."

Good accepted_position.details:

"The practical conclusion recognizes that the underlying mitzvah extends until dawn, while the Rabbis' midnight deadline functions as a protective enactment to prevent delay."

Use concise phrases.

Avoid long prose.

Use 1 to 3 sentences for most detail fields.
